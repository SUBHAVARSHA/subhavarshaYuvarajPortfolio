Sun Film Temperature Sensing Systems

The proposed system aimed to develop a sunfilm for car windows that effectively blocks ultraviolet (UV) radiation, thereby enhancing passenger comfort and safety during car travel. The method proposed involved simulating sunlight using an incandescent light bulb within a controlled environment, with temperature sensors integrated into the sunfilm to measure the heat generated by the simulated sunlight. An Arduino Uno R3 microcontroller was utilized to process the temperature readings, which were then displayed in real-time on an LCD screen for easy monitoring. The project comprised several key components, including the development of the UV- blocking sunfilm, construction of the experimental setup for simulating sunlight, integration of temperature sensors and an Arduino microcontroller, and implementation of an LCD display for data visualization. Calibration procedures were conducted to ensure the accuracy and reliability of temperature measurements under varying light intensities. Comprehensive testing evaluated the sunfilms performance under different light conditions, comparing temperature changes with baseline readings to assess its effectiveness in blocking UV radiation. The proposed method aimed at further enhancing the sunfilms effectiveness and applicability in mitigating UV exposure in automobiles.

Metaverse

The virtual worlds in the educational environment, have opened new possibilities to innovation in the teaching-learning process, where the flexibility of access to information inside or outside the classroom through an avatar, tends to a paradigm shift in education traditional, in terms of the way of transmitting and acquiring knowledge through technological means. In this sense, an investigation was carried out on the design, development and use of a metaverse as a digital tool of didactic support to students of the Faculty of Systems Engineering of the University of Cundinamarca, with a view to establishing its viability to be implemented in a course of mathematics and demonstrate the possibilities offered by this type of resource to motivate the student in his training. The metaverse design included various ICT tools, thereby promoting self-learning and collaborative learning. Finally, it was evaluated what was the appreciation of a group of students against their interaction with the metaverse, which validated its usefulness and functionality inside and outside the classroom.

Advanced Footsteps Power Generation Systems

The project advanced footstep power generator using RFID for charging describes when applying weight on piezoelectric plates voltage is developed across the plates. That voltage is applied to the battery for charging purposes. This is then provided to our monitoring circuitry.LCD is interfaced with a piezo sensor using a microcontroller that allows the user to monitor the voltage and charges a connected battery by it. Also, RFID is interfaced with microcontroller to know authorized users & it consists of a USB mobile phone charging point where the user may connect cables to charge the mobile phone from the battery charge.
